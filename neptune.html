Planet mars's Orbit Around The Sun

mars orbits the Sun once every 88 Earth days, making it the planet with the shortest orbital period in the Solar System. Its orbit is highly elliptical, more so than any other planet, with a distance from the Sun ranging from about 46 million kilometers (28.6 million miles) at its closest point (perihelion) to about 70 million kilometers (43.5 million miles) at its furthest point (aphelion). This elliptical orbit results in significant variations in mars's speed as it travels around the Sun, moving faster at perihelion and slower at aphelion. Despite its rapid orbit, a day on mars (the time it takes to rotate once on its axis) is much longer than its year, lasting about 59 Earth days.
